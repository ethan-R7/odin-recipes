Bimbimbap Recipe

Bimbimbap is a korean dish which includes a variety of vegetables, some meat and topped off with an raw egg served in a hot stone bowl.

Ingredients: For the Bibimbap Sauce (Gochujang Sauce): 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red pepper paste) 1 tablespoon soy sauce 1 tablespoon sesame oil 1 tablespoon sugar 1 clove garlic, minced 1 teaspoon sesame seeds For the Bibimbap Bowl: 2 cups cooked short-grain white rice (preferably Korean or Japanese rice) 1 cup spinach, blanched and seasoned 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched and seasoned 1 cup julienned carrots, sautéed 1 cup julienned cucumber, seasoned 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, sautéed 1/2 pound thinly sliced beef, marinated (see marinade below) 2 eggs Toasted sesame seeds (for garnish) Sesame oil (for drizzling) Vegetable oil (for frying eggs) Salt and pepper to taste For the Beef Marinade: 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon sugar 1 clove garlic, minced 1 teaspoon grated ginger 1 teaspoon sesame oil Instructions: Prepare all your vegetables, blanch the spinach and bean sprouts, and sauté the carrots, cucumber, and shiitake mushrooms separately. Season each with a pinch of salt and set aside. In a small bowl, mix all the ingredients for the Bibimbap sauce (Gochujang Sauce). Adjust the seasoning to taste. Set aside. Marinate the beef by combining all the marinade ingredients in a bowl and mixing well. Let it sit for about 10-15 minutes. Heat a pan over medium-high heat and add a bit of vegetable oil. Sauté the marinated beef until it's cooked through. Set aside. In the same pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up or to your preferred doneness. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. To assemble the Bibimbap bowls, start with a serving of cooked rice at the bottom of each bowl. Arrange the sautéed vegetables, cooked beef, and fried egg on top of the rice in separate sections to create a visually appealing presentation. Drizzle some sesame oil over the top and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds. Serve the Bibimbap with the Gochujang sauce on the side, allowing diners to add sauce to taste. Encourage them to mix everything together before eating to enjoy the full flavors of Bibimbap. Feel free to customize your Bibimbap with other vegetables or protein sources that you like. It's a versatile dish that can be adapted to your preferences. Enjoy your homemade Bibimbap!

Fish and Chips Recipe

Fish and chips is a classic British dish consisting of battered and fried fish served with crispy potato fries.

Ingredients:

For the Fish:

  • 4 cod fillets (or any white fish of your choice)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water (or cold beer for extra crispiness)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Bibimbap Bowl:

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain white rice (preferably Korean or Japanese rice)
  • 1 cup spinach, blanched and seasoned
  • 1 cup bean sprouts, blanched and seasoned
  • 1 cup julienned carrots, sautéed
  • 1 cup julienned cucumber, seasoned
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, sautéed
  • 1/2 pound thinly sliced beef, marinated (see marinade below)
  • 2 eggs
  • Toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Sesame oil (for drizzling)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying eggs)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Chips (Fries)::

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

Peel the potatoes and cut them into thick, uniform strips, about 1/2 inch wide.

Rinse the potato strips under cold water to remove excess starch, then pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.

Heat a large pot or deep fryer with vegetable oil to 325-350°F (163-177°C).

Carefully add the potato strips in batches to avoid overcrowding the oil. Fry for about 4-6 minutes or until they are lightly golden and starting to crisp up. Remove the fries with a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels.

Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C).

Return the partially fried potato strips to the hot oil in batches and fry for another 2-4 minutes or until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove them again and drain on paper towels. Season with salt immediately.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.

Gradually whisk in the cold sparkling water (or cold beer) until you have a smooth batter. The batter should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon but not too thick.

Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large heavy-bottomed pan to 350-375°F (177-190°C).

Dip each fish fillet into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off.

Carefully place the battered fish fillets into the hot oil. Fry for about 5-7 minutes or until they are golden brown and the fish is cooked through. The cooking time may vary depending on the thickness of your fillets.

Remove the fried fish using a slotted spoon and drain them on a paper towel-lined plate.

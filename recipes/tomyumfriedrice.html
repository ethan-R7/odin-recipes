Tomyum Fried Rice Recipe

Tom Yum Fried Rice is a flavorful Thai dish that combines the bold and tangy flavors of Tom Yum soup with the comfort of fried rice.

Ingredients:

For the Bibimbap Sauce (Gochujang Sauce):

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably cold and day-old)
  • 200g (7 oz) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g (7 oz) chicken breast, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 bird's eye chili peppers, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 tablespoons Tom Yum paste (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

Prepare your ingredients, ensuring the rice is cold and separated.

If using chicken, slice it thinly and marinate in soy sauce for 10-15 minutes. Combine fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and lime juice in a small bowl to make a sauce.

Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of oil. Stir-fry the chicken until no longer pink; then remove and set aside. Add another tablespoon of oil and cook the shrimp until pink and opaque; remove and set aside.

In the same pan, add minced garlic and bird's eye chili peppers. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add chopped onion and bell peppers, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until vegetables begin to soften.

Push vegetables to one side and add Tom Yum paste to the other side. Stir for about a minute to release flavor.

Add cold, cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry to combine with vegetables and Tom Yum paste.

Pour the prepared sauce over the rice and stir to evenly distribute the flavors. Add cherry tomatoes, frozen peas, cooked chicken (if using), and cooked shrimp. Continue to stir-fry until everything is heated through.

Taste and adjust seasoning with more fish sauce, soy sauce, or Tom Yum paste as needed.

Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve with lime wedges on the side. Enjoy your homemade Tom Yum Fried Rice with its bold and tangy Thai flavors!
